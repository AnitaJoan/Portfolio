Overview

Kpop Ecommerce Web Redesign - Mobile First

Timeline:      May - Current

Role:      Solo Project

Kpop fans struggle to find trustworthy, reliable sources to purchase official kpop albums from. Through surveys and interviews I understood 75% of customers only purchase from specific kpop artists they are devoted to.

With ideas gained from rapid sketching and a Value Proposition Canvas, I am currently testing a mid-fi prototype. My hypothesis is by creating an interactive, truly personalised online shopping experience that celebrates fandom and content, Ktown4u will become the go-to website for international fans.

My approach

Generally following the Double Diamond Process, I relied on sketches, affinity maps, assumptions canvas and user flows to visualise data and my thoughts. After some interviews I found more assumptions to validate and topics to expand upon with further interviews.

Being an international kpop fan is navigating barriers

The core difference between kpop fandoms and other musical genres is the level of time investment in content. Engaging with kpop music, beautiful music videos, reality shows and interactive fan meetings is a kpop fans guilty pleasure. As a result, kpop physical album sales are booming.

"Sometimes I feel like I'm wasting money but I remember that I've loved this group for 7 years and they mean a lot to me so I continue to support them through thick and thin"

PAINPOINTS

International fans face language barriers, different timezones and social isolation. When purchasing albums, they deal with exhorbitant shipping costs, long waits and unofficial merchandise.

Ktown4u.com

Ktown4u, a current kpop ecommerce, has the potential to be the biggest 'Korea to International' supplier but lacks in clear, friendly communication and doesn’t match the mental model of an ecommerce website in the west. (Ask me why!)

There are fan made videos on YouTube explaining how to navigate the website and make a successful purchase.

The website places too much emphasis on warning text, essentially scolding the customer before they’ve made a purchase. If the customer makes it to check out, they are overwhelmed with 8 shipping options and more warnings, impacting their trustworthiness.

Challenges

Find a unique, targeted approach to personalisation

Create a website experience to reduce the pain of shipping costs

Position the website as sharing the excitement of kpop

How might we make the wait the most exciting part?

While I can’t change the distance or shipping time, I could change how it was perceived. Ktown4u is unique in that they have connections to the artists and agencies for promotional purchases. From a design studio, I had an idea to intermittently send customers personal messages from their artists.

During testing, this concept was received positively but users didn’t believe it was a real message - instead auto-generated by the website. I’m currently considering: should I work on crafting authenticity or expand on the idea?

How might we target personalisation?

From research, I found that 75% of customers visit the website to buy a specific album by a specific artist. With the level of devotion the fans have, it doesn’t make sense to market them similar artists merchandise. Content had to be relevant to their interests.

During testing, I found 50% of users add the artists to favourites. When I asked them what it meant, none were completely sure. I might need to change the location of this to elsewhere in the user flow.

How might we celebrate collections?

Kpop albums are a collectable and full of lots of exclusive goodies. I have an idea to celebrate album collections and offer subscriptions to artist merchandise. This feature was left out of my first round of testing but I have since re-prioritised it for the next round.
