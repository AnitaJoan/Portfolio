Gamification of the British Museum Experience

CONCEPT - 2 WEEK SPRINT - 4 PERSON TEAM

Brief

The British Museum is one of the most popular and comprehensive museums in the world, attracting nearly 6 million visitors a year.

Our task was to make visiting the museum a highly engaging experience that helped visitors learn more about artefacts they liked and interact with exhibits. Visitors must also be able to track what they have enjoyed and share those discoveries on social media.

Process & Deliverables

    Discover
  • Site Visit
  • Competitive Analysis
  • User Interviews x13
  • Data Synthesis
    Define

    Develop

    Deliver
  • Hi-Fi Mockup
  • Clickable Prototype
  • 20min Presentation
  • Spec Document

Solution

Through observation of how visitors currently engage with the museum and several interviews, we were about to identify that interactivity and short bursts of content were the key to engagement.

We turned the museum experience into an educational scavenger hunt with a mobile game app. We converted heavy historical content into quick facts and connected them with a related storyline. At the end, we rewarded the user with an exclusive, museum themed photo filter which very exciting for users during testing.

Overview of Key Deliverables

Site Visit

To understand how people interact with the exhibits currently.

Top 3 insights
  • Most wander around
  • Desire to read dwindles over time
  • Take photos of things they like
Data Synthesis

To consolidate raw data and identify trends.

Top 3 insights
  • Value story & interaction
  • Prefer a linear journey
  • Too much to read
Design Studio

To rapidly generate design ideas.

Top 3 ideas
  • Fun facts scavenger hunt
  • Photo bingo
  • 3D interactive artefact view
Prototype

To test redesigns with users and present the concept.

Software used
  • Marvel for paper prototypes
  • Sketch for mid and hi-fi designs
  • InVision for final prototype
