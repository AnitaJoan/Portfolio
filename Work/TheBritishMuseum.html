Overview

Museum Companion Game App - Android

Timeline:      2 weeks

Role:      4 person team - User Interviews, data synthesis and project management

For our third project at General Assembly, we were tasked with creating an engaging app experience to use at the British Museum. The trends in our research showed interactivity and linear storylines to be most valued by museum visitors.

After 2 design studios, we decided to gamify the learning experience and turn a visit to the museum into a scavenger hunt for related artefacts. We believed by unifying artefacts with a storyline, visitors would be able to learn more about and discover what interests them most in the museum.

Our approach

We went to the British Museum and observed and spoke to people to understand how they already enjoy the exhibits. Our original agreed upon solution from our first design studio felt a little lack-luster the next day, so we conducted another and were excited by the game concept.

PAINPOINTS

Key frustrations were lack of linear journey closely followed by too much text to read. We observed this on a site visit where visitors engagement with reading artefact descriptions dropped as time went on.

The Concept Brief

Interact with the exhibits in some way. Learn more about artefacts they liked. Track what visitors have enjoyed and share those discoveries on social media. Make recommendations based on visitors profiles.

We found that visitors generally experienced one (or all) of the highlighted scenarios above. The key takeaway was that visitors needed to be able to locate and learn more about the artefacts that interested them most, as easily as possible.

Challenges

Our limited resources for interviews and testing meant our solution would be biased towards 20-30 year olds.

Testing our solution in the classroom and not the museum could skew results.

We had just 2 weeks and so focused on a proof of concept.

How might we connect artefacts into a linear storyline?

After 2 design studios, we decided to gamify the experience, connecting 10-20 artefacts together in a scavenger hunt for facts. Users could select the period of history most interesting to them and work with a friend to solve the mystery.

How might we encourage social sharing?

Inspired by Snapchat, we included exclusive museum themed photo filters that only unlocked upon the successful completion of the game storyline. This tested really positively with one user stating:

"I'm absolutely sharing this on instagram!"

How might we ensure users create an account?

As per the brief, it was important to be able to track visitors interests and recommended new exhibits. We found placing the sign up after the completion of the first game to be most effective, directly after users had understood the value in the game.

Outcome

The result of this project was a proof of concept. It was recieved very well during testing and I believe the photo sharing would have encouraged more visitors to return and 'complete the museum'. With the sign up data, we could have understood the most popular artefacts and built stories around them.
